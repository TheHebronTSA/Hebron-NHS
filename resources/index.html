    How can I get hours?

    Hours can be obtained by the following:

     

    1) signing up for events via the Remind or Twitter

    2) discovering your own service opportunities

     

    Service opportunities announced through Twitter or the Remind are considered NHS events. You must partake in one NHS event per semester. Some examples of these events include the Block Lunch events and volunteering for the CCA Food Pantry.

     

    When discovering your own service opportunities, they must be pre-approved.  That means you MUST get it approved prior to partaking in that service activity. Below is a list of Hebron NHS Approved Individual Service Opportunities. However, you are not limited to this list. If the service opportunity that you have discovered is not on this list, email Mrs. Bowen at or Ms. Perkins at to get it approved!

    How many hours do I need?

    Juniors: 60 hours and 2 NHS events (one per semester) by the end of the school year. Your goal should be to attain 15 hours per nine weeks. You will only be penalized if you do not submit any hours. 

     

    Seniors: 50 hours and 2 NHS events (one per semester) by the end of April. Your goal should be to attain around 12.5 hours per nine weeks. You will only be penalized if you do not submit any hours.

    ​

    How do summer hours work?

    We encourage all NHS members to volunteer as much as they would like over the summer; however, only 20 hours will be recorded to fulfill NHS hour requirements.

     

    Summer hours count towards yearly NHS hour requirements. So if you're a junior and you attain 20 hours during the summer, then by the end of the school year, you only have to submit 40 additional hours.

     

    Many NHS members choose to partake in mission trips over the summer. Mission trip volunteer work is encouraged; however, time spent eating, sleeping, or proselytizing will not be counted towards NHS hour requirements. 

     

    Volunteering for Bible School will also not be counted towards NHS hour requirements. While we applaud your efforts to volunteer, we have to abide by the separation of church and state requirement through our national organization. 

    What are demerits?

    The Hebron NHS demerit system ensures that all members are participating in plentiful service opportunities throughout the year. You will only be penalized if you commit the following reprehensible crimes:

     

    1. You submit zero hours at the end of the nine weeks (2 demerits)

    2. You do not participate in an NHS event by the end of the semester (2 demerits)

    3. You miss three monthly meetings (1 demerit)

     

    If you receive three demerits, you will be dismissed from NHS.
